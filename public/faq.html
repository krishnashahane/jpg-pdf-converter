Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers about how ipdfjpg.com works, what we support, and how we protect your privacy.

Is the service free?

Yes. Core conversions are free for everyone. There are no premium-only output formats and no watermarks.

Do you store my files?

No. Files are processed temporarily to complete the conversion, then removed by automated cleanup. We do not inspect content or sell file data. Our Privacy Policy details the process and retention window.

What file sizes are supported?

Each upload supports up to 100MB. JPG to PDF accepts up to 20 images per batch. For smoother uploads, keep individual images between 500KB and 4MB; this balances speed and quality while avoiding browser timeouts.

Which devices and browsers work best?

ipdfjpg.com works on current versions of Chrome, Edge, Firefox, and Safari on desktop and mobile. Enable JavaScript and a stable network connection for the best experience. If you hit an error, refresh or try another browser first.

How is the service funded?

The service is free and supported by the community. We never sell your data.

How do I keep quality high?

Upload clear source files. For JPG to PDF, shoot documents in good light and crop them before uploading. For PDF to JPG, ensure your PDF uses crisp text and vector shapes where possible. We optimize output to preserve detail while keeping file sizes small.

My upload failed. What should I try?

Check file type (PDF or JPG only) and size (under 100MB). Confirm your connection is stable, then retry in a fresh tab. If the issue persists, visit our Contact page with the browser, device, and approximate file size so we can investigate.

Is there a page limit?

JPG to PDF is limited to 20 images per batch to ensure speed. PDF to JPG converts the first page, ideal for covers and previews. To convert another page, reorder your PDF so the desired page is first and run the conversion again.

Can I use the tools for business?

Yes. Agencies, freelancers, schools, and businesses use ipdfjpg.com daily. For sensitive documents, remove unnecessary pages before uploading and delete local downloads after sharing. We recommend using HTTPS-only networks when handling confidential material.
