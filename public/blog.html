Tips on working with PDFs and images.

PDF to JPG without losing clarity

Prepare your PDF at 150-300 DPI for best results. The converter renders the first page to a neutral white canvas and compresses to ~1200x1600 px, keeping text legible and gradients smooth. Use sRGB color profiles for consistent output. For newsletters, aim for 120-400 KB per image.

Batch JPG to PDF for clean submissions

Photograph documents in even light, crop edges, and rename files with a sequence (01, 02, 03) to maintain order. The converter normalizes to ~2480x3508 px (A4 at 300 DPI). No watermarks are added. For best print results, select “Actual Size” in your PDF viewer.

Optimizing images for the web

Use 150 DPI for web/email, 300 DPI for print. Convert to sRGB for digital delivery. Strip EXIF metadata when file size matters. Test your output on mobile data to ensure fast loading.

Privacy tips for document conversion

Use HTTPS and trusted networks. Redact sensitive info before uploading. Clear downloads when done. Only upload files you have permission to process. Decline non-essential cookies via the banner if you prefer.

Print-ready PDFs from phone photos

Shoot on a flat surface in even light. Crop tight and rotate so text is upright. Use descriptive filenames with a sequence. Aim for 500 KB - 4 MB per JPG. Print a test page before the full run.
